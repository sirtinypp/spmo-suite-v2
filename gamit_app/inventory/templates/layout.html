System Knowledge Base

A contextual guide to GAMIT terminology and reporting standards.

RPCPPE Report on the Physical Count of Property, Plant and Equipment. The primary annual audit report.
PPE Property, Plant and Equipment. Tangible assets with high value (typically >₱50,000).
Serviceable Items in good working condition and officially ready for use in university operations.
Custodian The specific personnel or officer officially accountable for the physical asset.
Property Number The unique university-assigned tracking identifier used for all audit and movement logs.
Book Value The original acquisition cost minus accumulated depreciation (the current accounting net worth).
Acquisition Cost The total original price paid to purchase the asset, including taxes, delivery, and installation.
Salvage Value The estimated residual value of an asset at the end of its useful life (standard: 5% of cost).
Depreciation The systematic allocation of an asset's cost over its estimated useful life.
Depreciation Method The formula used to calculate loss of value (Standard University practice: Straight-Line Method).
Estimated Useful Life The period over which an asset is expected to be functional (e.g., ICT Equipment = 5 years).
Fair Market Value The estimated price the asset would sell for on the open market in its current condition.
Fund Source The specific budget source used for procurement (e.g., GAA, Trust Fund, or Grants).
Appraisal Value The expert-estimated worth of an asset.
Formula: (Replacement Cost New - Total Depreciation) + Adjustments.
Semi-Expendable Assets valued between ₱15,000 and ₱50,000; monitored but not fully capitalized.
Status (General) The current operational state of the asset (Serviceable, Unserviceable, Lost, or For Disposal).
Capitalization Threshold Policy All tangible assets with an acquisition cost of ₱50,000 and above must be recorded as Property, Plant and Equipment (PPE) and issued a PAR. Items below ₱50,000 are classified as Semi-Expendable Property and issued an ICS.
Property Transfer Policy Accountability transfer between officers requires an approved Property Transfer Report (PTR). The receiving officer assumes full responsibility upon signing.
Loss of Property Protocol Accountable officers must submit a Notice of Loss and a Notarized Affidavit of Loss within 30 days of the incident to initiate relief from accountability.
Disposal & Condemnation Guidelines Unserviceable property must undergo Pre-repair Inspection and be officially condemned via an IIRUP before physical disposal or public auction.
